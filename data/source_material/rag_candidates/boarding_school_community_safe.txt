A boarding school community brings academic life, residential life, and shared daily routines into one school setting. The purpose of that community is not only to hold classes, but also to help students practice responsibility, collaboration, independence, respect, and support for one another. Community language is often broad because it describes the values and habits that shape how students live and learn together.

This source does not name current staff, set current policies, describe dorm rules, or state dining procedures. It can support general answers about the purpose of a boarding school community, but it should not be used to answer questions about current people, schedules, discipline, phone rules, admissions deadlines, or operational details.
